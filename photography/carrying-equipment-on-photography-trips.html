Carrying Equipment on Photography Trips

The problem

I used to go on long walking-photography trips and for lack of better carrying solution, I just used my general-purpose laptop bag to carry the equipment. It kind of worked fine but it didn't have any sternum strap or a waist belt and carrying the weight of the equipment and the bag itself was rather uncomfortable. When I needed to go on cycling-photography trips, I used the same bag and because of the lack of waist belt, it swayed from side to side especially when pedaling while standing up, off the seat.

Solution

Any decent bag I found was either too expensive or too heavy or both. While cycling at least, I need to be as light as possible. When walking or travelling in general by other means, a (relatively) heavy bag isn't much of a problem. I was looking at Kata Beetle for some time but it was too expensive to be a feasible option. But one day, casually browsing, I found it at a ridiculously low price on some online store (70% off!) so I immediately ordered it and apart from being a bit heavy, I am quite happy with it for most uses including carrying the full load including a tripod (and even a laptop, water etc.

Solution for cycling

Although that still was too heavy if I was just carrying one or two lenses and the camera body. A few options I found at MEC store were all too expensive for my requirements. But then I had a wonderful idea! A drawstring bag does it! It's really cheap, easy to find a relatively waterproof one, and I could keep the body and lenses in a smaller casing inside the drawstring bag to protect against shock, moisture etc. I use a small carabiner to prevent the bag from hanging and swaying when biking and it works perfectly. It is virtually weightless and has pockets which I can use to keep different lenses in or my gloves, water bottles etc.
